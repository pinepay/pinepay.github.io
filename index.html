Our story

High Agency: Pine Pay is betting on mobile, with belief and persistency

All companies start out with a belief that they can change the status quo. They believe they can create something better - process, service, product, whatever. That they can delight, optimise, save time or money; that they can create value. This is the premise of progress, the leap of faith, the fuel that drives founders and creators.

Building new things is objectively good, itâ€™s what drives progress and innovation. Bad products are discarded and ignored, and bad companies quickly fail. And not all good products survive. Most of the time, new needs to be provided to people, stubbornly and persistently. Successful founders need grit to create innovation. They have to persuade, inspire, and most importantly believe that there's will prevail.

Pine Pay is taking a bet on mobile payments. What we believe is that mobile payments (integrated with open banking and powered by tailored rewards) are better. Cash, credit cards and fees will soon be unnecessary in a world full of smartphones and digital bank accounts. It seems a safe bet. Just look at Asia. The unknown is how long it will take to convince people of this, and if folks will choose our platform (to this, see our response: Why not Pine Pay?).

High agency can be thought of as follows:

Life can be much broader once you discover one simple fact: Everything around you that you call life was made up by people that were no smarter than you and you can change it, you can influence it, you can build your own things that other people can use.

Steve Jobs

Once you learn that, you'll never be the same again.

High agency is this belief that you can effect change, affect lives. This belief is really all that is required to do something exceptional. Resilient, unwavering, unrelenting belief. This is not only a message for founders, but for anyone, doing anything. Your contributions can matter if you believe they do. What is more inspiring than that?
